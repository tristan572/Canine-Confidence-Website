It was great working with you and Simba today! Below is a summary of what we covered and some key reminders to continue practicing at home.

1. Leash Pressure
Keep the leash loose at all times unless you’re actively communicating with Simba.
When you want him to move in a particular direction, tighten the leash slightly and pull gently in that direction.
As soon as Simba moves with the pressure or reaches the position you want, release the pressure immediately — the release itself is his reward.
You can also use nagging pressure (a light “tap, tap, tap” on the leash) when Simba is ignoring a known command. The nagging stops as soon as he complies.
Always ensure Simba understands what you’re asking before using nagging pressure.
2. Loose Lead Walking (“Let’s Go”)
“Let’s go” means Simba must move with you — no sniffing, no pulling.
If Simba sniffs after you’ve said “let’s go,” mark with “No” and give a couple of small pops on the leash (a quick on-and-off motion).
If he pulls, mark “No” and apply a few quick pops as a reminder.
To release him for sniffing or exploration, say “Free.” During “Free,” he can sniff and move about, but must not pull. Move with him to prevent tension on the leash.
Always maintain a loose leash, even during “Free.”
Training Tip:
Start with short intervals — brief “Let’s Go” periods followed by “Free” breaks. Gradually increase the time spent on “Let’s Go” and reduce the amount of “Free” time. Simba will naturally begin working harder in hopes of earning “Free.”
3. Equipment Use
Continue using the flat collar and flexi lead (especially in open spaces or parks).
Even with the flexi lead, don’t allow pulling.
“Let’s Go” should always mean move with you, regardless of which lead or collar Simba is wearing.
You’re both doing great — keep practicing these techniques in low-distraction environments before adding challenges like other dogs or busier settings.
